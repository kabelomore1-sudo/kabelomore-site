## The Strategic Frame

You're not running 5 audits per week. You're operating a **factory that
turns each audit into 5+ assets**:

| Audit produces | Asset | Compounds |
|----------------|-------|-----------|
| 1 | PDF report → prospect | No (single-use, but conversion event) |
| 2 | Notion CRM row | Yes (funnel data) |
| 3 | `/learnings/` markdown file | Yes (specific knowledge IP) |
| 4 | LinkedIn post (anonymised) | Yes (AI training data + lead gen) |
| 5 | Leaderboard entry (anonymised) | Yes (Index authority) |
| 6 | Newsletter content fragment | Yes (nurture asset) |
| 7 | Case study candidate flag | Yes (medium-term trust artifact) |

5 audits × 7 outputs = **35 weekly assets**. Plus 1 newsletter issue,
1 hike-derived strategic insight, and (sometimes) 1 blog post draft =
**~38 assets per week.**

The point isn't the audit. The point is what the audit *generates.*

## Day 0 — Pre-Week Setup (Sunday afternoon)

**2 hours, completed before Monday morning.**

### 1. Identify 5 prospects (45 min)

Sector mix matters more than total count:
- 2 medical practices (1 Pretoria, 1 Joburg or Cape Town)
- 2 industrial firms (1 BBBEE Level 1-2, 1 mining-adjacent)
- 1 law firm (mid-market commercial)

**Why mix sectors**: pattern recognition emerges across sectors faster
than within. Single-sector batching reveals less.

**Sources for prospect identification:**
- LinkedIn search: `"[specialty] in [city]"` — top 2-3 candidates
- Google Maps: `"best [service] [city]"` — top 2 visible firms
- HPCSA registry (medical), LSSA directory (legal), CSD database (industrial)
- Industry publications' supplier directories (Engineering News, Medical Brief)
- Existing personal network — past colleagues, alumni, family connections

### 2. Send 5 free scan offers (60 min)

Personalized DM/email template:

```
Hi [NAME],

Saw [SPECIFIC THING — recent post, hospital affiliation, project win,
news mention]. Quick offer.

I'm running 5 free AI Visibility Scans this week to validate a
methodology I've been documenting publicly. The scan tests how ChatGPT,
Claude, Gemini, and Perplexity respond when [PATIENTS / CLIENTS /
PROCUREMENT OFFICERS] search for [SPECIALTY] in [CITY].

24-hour turnaround. No card. No follow-up sales.

Just want to see how SA [SECTOR] firms are doing across AI engines.

Want one for [FIRM NAME]?

— Kabelo
kabelomore.com/scan
```

**Hook formula**: specific personalization + clear value + low
commitment + named context.

**Target**: 3 'yes' responses from 5 sent. If fewer, send 5 more by
Monday morning.

### 3. Set up Notion CRM (15 min)

One database. These fields:

```
- Business name
- Contact name
- Sector (Medical / Legal / Industrial)
- City
- Date scan requested
- Date scan delivered
- AEO score (0-100)
- Top finding
- Recommendation tier (Foundation / Optimization / Lite / Local Growth / etc.)
- Conversion outcome (call scheduled / declined / ghost / paid retainer)
- Anonymised name for public use (e.g. "Practice F")
- Public consent (Yes / No / Pending)
```

### 4. Calendar block

Lock Mon-Sun blocks now. Don't let other work creep in.

## The 7-Day Calendar

### Monday — Audit 1 + LinkedIn launch

**8-9am — Cafe in Brooklyn or Menlyn**
- Coffee + read industry news for Audit 1's sector (Medical Brief / De
  Rebus / Engineering News / BizCommunity).
- Capture 1 quotable observation. Useful for Tuesday's post.

**9-12pm — Audit 1**
- Run full 5-stage process per `AUDIT-PLAYBOOK.md`
- Stage 1 (15 min): pre-audit intake
- Stage 2 (10 min wait): automated scan running
- Stage 3 (90 min): manual deep audit across 7 properties
- Stage 4 (30 min): synthesis + tier recommendation
- Stage 5 (15 min): delivery + learning capture

**1-2pm — Lunch + reset**

**2-3pm — LinkedIn post #1 (Monday template)**
- Use the audit-insight template from `LINKEDIN-PLAYBOOK.md`:
  *"Just audited a [SECTOR] firm in [CITY]. Their AEO score was [X]/100..."*
- Anonymised. Specific findings.
- Reply to comments for 30 min after publishing.

**3-4pm — Documentation**
- Add Audit 1 to Notion CRM
- Create `/learnings/2026-MM-DD-{business}.md` (anonymised file name OK)
- Stage anonymised entry for leaderboard (Sunday batch)

### Tuesday — Audit 2 + Quote-of-the-day

**Morning — Cafe (8-12pm) — Audit 2**
- Different sector from Monday. Forces cross-sector pattern thinking.

**Afternoon (1-3pm) — LinkedIn post #2**
- Tuesday template: quotable quick take, drawn from Mon + Tue audits.
- Format: ONE sharp sentence + attribution. No CTA. Let it spread.
- Example: *"The single biggest AEO blocker for SA medical practices
  isn't schema. It's that 70% don't have HelloPeter profiles claimed."
  — pattern across 8 medical audits in May.*

**3-4pm — Documentation**
- Same routine as Monday.

### Wednesday — HIKE DAY (Magaliesberg) + Audit 3

**6-10am — Magaliesberg hike**

The first synthesis pause of the week.

**Pre-hike (night before)**:
- Write 2 specific questions on a notecard:
  - "What pattern do I see across Audits 1 + 2?"
  - "What's the ONE thing that would shift if all 3 firms fixed it?"
- Tuck card in pack.
- Bring small notebook + single pen. **Leave laptop. Phone on airplane mode.**

**During hike**:
- Walking pace = thinking pace.
- Don't force insight. Let it come.
- No podcasts. No music. No phone-checking.

**Summit / mid-hike pause (15-20 min)**:
- Sit. Drink water.
- Write whatever's in your head — raw, unedited, one sentence per thought.
- Find the Naval-style quotable line.

**Post-hike (30 min at home/cafe)**:
- Type up the notes.
- Identify ONE quotable insight.
- Take a photo of the trail/summit/landscape.

**12-4pm — Cafe (Brooklyn) — Audit 3**
- Run with the hike pattern in mind. Confirm or break the hypothesis.

**4-6pm — LinkedIn post #3 (Wednesday template)**
- Methodology breakdown that USES the hike insight.
- Pair with the Magaliesberg photo.
- *"The lifestyle IS the marketing"* — Naval's frame visible: a London
  consultant cannot fake "thinking on a hike in the Magaliesberg cradle
  of humankind." The place-specificity is the moat.

### Thursday — Newsletter Issue 1 + Audit 4

**8-11am — Newsletter Issue 1 writing**

This is the synthesis distribution layer. The first real issue of The
AEO Letter (after Issue 0 / welcome).

Title pattern: *"What I learned auditing 3 SA firms this week."*

Structure (200-400 words):
- Pattern from Audit 1 (anonymised, specific number)
- Pattern from Audit 2 (anonymised, specific number)
- Pattern from Audit 3 (anonymised, specific number)
- The cross-cutting insight from Wednesday's Magaliesberg hike
- One specific fix every reader can do today
- CTA: free scan link

Add to `lib/newsletter-issues.ts` as a new entry. Triggers Resend send
in the morning per the cadence promise.

**1-4pm — Audit 4**

**4-6pm — LinkedIn post #4 (Thursday template)**
- Newsletter teaser format. Drives traffic to the public Issue 1 URL.
- Public + ungated by design — every share = AI training data.

### Friday — Audit 5 + week wrap

**8-12pm — Audit 5 (final)**

**1-3pm — Synthesis across all 5**
- Pull together patterns. What's true across sectors? What's specific?
- Stage 5-7 entries for leaderboard pipeline.

**3-4pm — LinkedIn post #5 (Friday template)**
- Sector-specific tip drawn from one audit.
- Format: *"If you're a [SECTOR] firm in SA, do this in 15 minutes..."*
- Link to relevant `/resources/quick-wins/{sector}` page.

**4-5pm — Prospect close-out emails**
- Deliver any PDFs not yet sent.
- Schedule any follow-up calls.
- Closing email asking for permission to anonymise + publish learnings:

```
Hi [NAME], your scan PDF is attached. Quick ask.

I'm publishing anonymised pattern observations from this week's 5 audits
on kabelomore.com/newsletter. Your firm appears as "Practice F" or
"Firm B" — no name, no city specifics. The patterns are useful for the
sector at large.

OK with that, or prefer not? Either is fine. — Kabelo
```

### Saturday — LONG HIKE (big synthesis)

**7am-12pm — Magaliesberg longer trail (or rotate to Hennops, Suikerbosrand, Walter Sisulu)**

The bigger synthesis. Take 3 questions:

1. "What is the ONE thing all 5 firms have in common as a gap?"
2. "Which sector has the biggest opportunity? Why?"
3. "What needs to change in my methodology / pricing / positioning
   based on what I learned this week?"

Same rules: phone airplane mode, journal + pen at the end.

**This is where strategic shifts happen.** Naval: "Hikes are leverage
on your best thinking time. Compound your judgment, not just your
output."

**1-3pm — Capture insights**
- Type up notebook contents at home or cafe.
- Identify the BIG insight of the week (one paragraph).
- Draft Monday's "next week" LinkedIn post hook based on it.
- Update `/learnings/index.md` with the week's pattern recognition.
- If a CASE STUDY emerged (someone converting + showing fast results):
  start the case study draft using OMS Lifting structure.

### Sunday — Rest + Sunday batch

**Morning: REST.** No work. Family / non-work activity. Recovery is
part of the rhythm — not separate.

**2-5pm — Sunday batch (per `LINKEDIN-PLAYBOOK.md` cadence)**:

```
[ ] Write next week's Mon-Fri LinkedIn posts (5 posts, batched)
[ ] Update leaderboard with this week's 5 entries (anonymised — wait
    for explicit consent before naming)
[ ] Update /learnings/index.md
[ ] Tally CRM funnel: how many of 5 converted? Pattern from outcomes?
[ ] Identify next week's 5 prospects + draft their outreach DMs
[ ] Block next week's calendar
[ ] Prep next Wednesday + Saturday hike notecards
```

## Why Each Platform, Why Each Output

### 1. Audit PDF → Prospect
- **Why**: revenue event. Their decision driver.
- **Audience**: the prospect specifically.
- **Tone**: professional, firm-specific, actionable.
- **Compounds**: No (single-use, but it's the conversion artifact).

### 2. Notion CRM
- **Why**: track funnel rates. Without this you can't tell which audits
  convert and why.
- **Audience**: future you.
- **Compounds**: Yes — pattern data accrues over time.

### 3. `/learnings/` files
- **Why**: pattern-recognition source. Becomes blog posts, newsletters,
  the eventual book.
- **Audience**: future you + collaborators when you hire.
- **Compounds**: Massively — 60 audits × 6 months = a body of work no
  SA competitor has.

### 4. LinkedIn post (anonymised)
- **Why**: AI engines harvest LinkedIn heavily. Each post = direct
  training data injection. By month 6, "AEO consultant SA" queries on
  ChatGPT/Perplexity surface Kabelo More by name.
- **Audience**: prospects who haven't found you yet.
- **Compounds**: Yes — every post is permanent + searchable + AI-citable.

### 5. Leaderboard pipeline
- **Why**: every entry deepens the SA AEO Index. After 50 entries, the
  Index becomes the public benchmark — the role is hard to replace.
- **Audience**: industry observers, journalists, future buyers.
- **Compounds**: Massively — the Index is the defensive moat.

### 6. Newsletter content bank
- **Why**: subscribers convert at 4-12 weeks of nurture. The Letter is
  your long-tail conversion engine.
- **Audience**: subscribers (mid-funnel).
- **Compounds**: Yes — every issue indexed by AI engines forever.

### 7. Case study candidates
- **Why**: 1-2 named case studies = 5× faster conversion of future
  prospects who land on /case-studies.
- **Audience**: future prospects in the discovery phase.
- **Compounds**: Yes — durable trust artifact for years.

## The Hike Integration Playbook

The Magaliesberg pattern (or your local trail of choice).

### Pre-hike (5 min the night before)

```
[ ] Write 1-2 specific questions on a notecard
[ ] Tuck card in pack
[ ] DO NOT pack laptop
[ ] DO pack: water, journal, single pen, snack, sunscreen
```

### During hike

- Phone on airplane mode (or leave at home if Magaliesberg isn't a
  comms-blackout zone).
- Walking pace = thinking pace.
- No podcasts, no audiobooks, no music.
- Don't force insight. The brain works on its own when given time.
- Notice physical sensations — heart rate, breath, terrain. Brings
  you back to the body and out of the head-loop.

### Summit / mid-hike pause (15-20 min)

```
[ ] Sit. Drink water. Eat snack.
[ ] Open notebook
[ ] Write whatever's in your head — raw, unedited
[ ] One sentence per thought. No paragraphs yet.
[ ] Look for the ONE quotable line that surprises you
```

### Post-hike (30 min at home/cafe)

```
[ ] Type up notebook contents
[ ] Identify ONE quotable insight worth a LinkedIn post
[ ] Save raw notes to /learnings/hikes/2026-MM-DD-{trail}.md
[ ] Take 1 photo from the day → social use
[ ] Draft the LinkedIn post pairing photo + insight
```

### The compounding loop

```
Hike thinking
    ↓
Quotable line captured
    ↓
LinkedIn post (paired with hike photo)
    ↓
Buyer reads it (week 1)
    ↓
Buyer requests free scan (week 2)
    ↓

    ↓

    ↓

    ↓

    ↓

```

## Anti-Patterns to Avoid

1. **Skipping the documentation.** The audit feels productive even
   without it. It's the documentation that compounds — without it,
   you're just doing consulting hours.

2. **Skipping the hike.** Wednesday's hike feels skippable when an
   audit overruns. **Don't skip it.** The synthesis is what makes the
   week worth more than just 5 audits.

3. **Posting reactively on LinkedIn.** If you write Mon-Fri posts in
   the moment, you'll burn out. Sunday batch + small daily edits =
   sustainable cadence.

4. **Optimizing one audit too much.** Run all 5 first to find the
   pattern. Don't over-engineer Audit 1 and skip 2-5.

5. **Pitching retainers in LinkedIn posts.** Pitch the methodology +
   the free resource. Pitches kill share rate. Naval: "Sell after they
   show up."

6. **Not asking for case study consent.** When a prospect is happy with
   the scan, ask THEN for permission to anonymise + publish learning.
   Cold-asking later is harder.

7. **Letting the cadence drift.** Status updates every 3 days. Mon
   newsletter every Thursday. LinkedIn every weekday. The cadence IS
   the moat as much as the content.

8. **Not tracking funnel data.** Without CRM rows you can't tell which
   sector converts, which post drives most prospects, which audit
   format works. Track or guess.
