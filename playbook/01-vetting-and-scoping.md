# 01 — Vetting & Scoping

How to qualify a prospect, decide which package fits, and quote them in 10 minutes after the free scan.

## The vetting principle

Most consulting failures aren't from bad delivery — they're from **bad client selection.** A wrong-fit client at R12,500 costs you more in scope creep and stress than a right-fit client at R5,000.

Vet ruthlessly. Walk away when red flags show up. The right client is more valuable than the right price.

---

## Stage 1 — When the free scan arrives

Every scan submission is a qualified lead until proven otherwise. Run this filter as you write the scan PDF:

### Green flags (lean in)

- Specific business name + verified website (or genuine "no website yet" status)
- Real industry (industrial, legal, medical, professional, B2B services)
- Geographic specificity ("Pretoria CBD" beats "South Africa")
- Real services listed (not "we do everything")
- Average customer worth R5,000+ to them (this matters for ROI math later)
- Decision-maker filled the form (their name, their email, not "info@" or "admin@")

### Red flags (caution)

- Personal Gmail with no last name
- Vague service description ("we help businesses grow")
- "I want to be #1 on ChatGPT" expectations (unrealistic and indicates they don't understand AEO)
- Requests for guarantees ("can you guarantee me 10 leads/month?")
- Already failed with 2+ marketing agencies in 12 months (often the issue is them, not the agencies)
- Wants to "start small and see" without committing to scope (scope creep risk)

### Walk-away triggers

- MLM / get-rich-quick / crypto scheme businesses
- Anyone trying to negotiate price before scan delivery
- Anyone asking for back-end / SEO blackhat work ("can you delete bad reviews?")
- Anyone whose business is harmful (gambling, payday loans, shady supplements)
- Anyone who's rude in initial communication (predicts how they'll be at month 3)

Decline politely: *"This isn't quite the right fit for what I do. I'd recommend [referral or alternative consultant]. Best of luck."*

## Stage 2 — Scan delivery + follow-up call

Send the scan PDF within 24 hours of submission. Follow up 48-72 hours later via WhatsApp:

> *Hey [Name], did the AI scan land OK? I noticed [one specific finding from their scan, e.g. "Perplexity didn't mention you for any of the 3 priority queries we tested"]. Want to talk through what to do next? Free 20-minute call, no pitch — just a walk-through.*

If they book the call, run this discovery flow.

### Discovery questions (the 8 that matter)

1. **"What does success look like in 6 months?"** — vague answers = vague scope. Push for specifics.
2. **"What's a single new customer worth to you, roughly?"** — drives ROI math for pricing justification.
3. **"What have you tried before for marketing — what worked, what failed?"** — surfaces past frustrations + scope of what to avoid.
4. **"Who else is in the buying decision?"** — if not them solely, you need that person on call 2.
5. **"What's your timeline pressure?"** — too urgent (this week) = bad fit. Too vague (someday) = won't close.
6. **"Walk me through your current website setup if you have one — platform, who built it, can you log in?"** — drives Foundation vs Optimization decision.
7. **"What's your monthly marketing budget all-in?"** — qualifies retainer fit.
8. **"On a scale of 1-10, how serious are you about being cited by AI engines specifically?"** — anything 7+ converts. <5 doesn't.

### The qualifying matrix

After discovery, score them on 4 dimensions:

| Dimension | Score 1-10 | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| Decision-maker on call | 10 = yes / 0 = needs approval from absent partner | |
| Specific 6-month goal | 10 = numerical / 5 = directional / 0 = vague | |
| Single customer value | R5k+ = 10 / R1-5k = 5 / under R1k = 0 | |
| Realistic expectations | 10 = understands AEO is compounding / 0 = wants instant ranking | |

**Total 30+** → Quote them.
**Total 20-29** → Pitch Starter Audit (R5,000) first. Earn the right to upsell.
**Total <20** → Decline politely. Refer elsewhere.

## Stage 3 — Choosing the right package

Once they pass the qualifying matrix, scope based on starting point:

### The decision tree

```
Q1: "Do you currently have a working website?"
    │
    ├─ NO  →  Q2: "Are you a sole trader or a small team?"
    │           ├─ Sole trader → FOUNDATION LITE (R6,500, 2 wks)
    │           └─ Team/limited co → FOUNDATION PACK (R12,500, 4 wks)
    │
    └─ YES → Q3: "Is the website slow / broken / built on dead tech?"
                ├─ YES, broken → FOUNDATION PACK rebuild (R12,500, 4 wks)
                ├─ NO, sole trader → OPTIMIZATION LITE (R5,500, 2 wks)
                └─ NO, normal business → OPTIMIZATION PACK (R10,500, 3 wks)

After once-off delivery, ALL clients get pitched:
GROWTH RETAINER (R8,500/mo, 3-month minimum) for ongoing compounding.
```

### Quick "just one piece" carve-outs

If a client wants only one specific thing, route them to the matching add-on. Don't try to upsell them to a full package — let them buy small, deliver well, earn the right to bigger work.

| Client says | Sell them | Price |
|---|---|---|
| "I just want my schema deployed" | Schema Pack add-on | R4,500 |
| "I just want my GBP done" | GBP Setup add-on | R3,500 |
| "I just need 50 citations built" | Citation Pack 50 | R6,500 |
| "I just want to know where I stand" | Starter Audit | R5,000 |

These are entry points. ~30% of one-piece buyers come back for the full package within 90 days.

## Stage 4 — Pricing scoping (turning a scan into a custom quote)

Standard packages are the floor. **Custom quotes scope up or down based on what the scan reveals.** Use this calculator:

### Base price (start here)

```
Existing site, normal business     →  R10,500 (Optimization Pack base)
Existing site, sole trader         →  R5,500  (Optimization Lite base)
No website, normal business        →  R12,500 (Foundation Pack base)
No website, sole trader            →  R6,500  (Foundation Lite base)
```

### Adjustments (add or subtract)

```
+ R2,500    if they need more than 10 citations (industry-specific dirs)
+ R2,500    if they need 5+ priority pages rewritten (vs default 3)
+ R2,500    if they need multilingual content (Afrikaans + English, etc.)
+ R3,500    if they want Discovery & Strategy Sprint bundled
+ R2,500    if they want Self-Edit Admin Panel installed
+ R1,500    if they're on a complex platform (Shopify with custom theme,
            HubSpot CMS, Webflow with multiple CMS collections)
+ R1,500    rush delivery (compress 3 weeks to 2 weeks, OR 4 weeks to 3)

- R1,000    if they only have 1 priority service (not 3)
- R1,500    if they don't need GBP setup (already 90%+ complete)
- R1,500    if they don't need new citations (already have 25+ consistent)
- R2,000    if they're a referral from an existing client
```

### International multiplier

If client is UK / US / EU:

```
SA price × 1.0 = SA Rand quote
SA price × 0.06 ≈ USD quote (use this as starting USD)
SA price × 0.045 ≈ GBP quote (use this as starting GBP)

Then verify against floor:
- USD floor for Optimization Pack: $795
- GBP floor for Optimization Pack: £595
- USD floor for Foundation Pack: $895
- GBP floor for Foundation Pack: £675

Never go below the floor. The floor is what /pricing publishes.
```

If the scoped quote comes out higher than the SA price would, charge the higher number — that's why we converted.

### Founder-pricing exception (first 5 clients)

For your first 5 paying clients in 2026, you can offer **40-50% off** in exchange for:

- Public case study rights (named usage on kabelomore.com)
- Before/after data shared (with their approval)
- 60-second video testimonial (phone-camera fine)
- Permission to track them in the daily AI tracker for 6 months

Document this in writing. Never founder-price without case study rights — that's the trade.

## Red flags that appear AFTER they've paid (catch these in week 1)

Even after deposit clears, watch for:

- They miss the brief deadline by more than 5 days → schedule a recovery call, escalate
- They want to add scope without paying for it → quote the add-on, don't absorb
- They go silent for >7 days during active work → status email, second email, then phone
- They start CC'ing partners they didn't mention → loop them in officially with a kickoff call
- They start comparing your work to "what their cousin would do" → educate (this post: AEO vs SEO blog)

The earlier you catch a wobbling client, the cheaper the recovery. Most engagement disasters were obvious in week 1 and ignored until week 3.

## What happens at delivery (the upsell moment)

Every once-off delivery ends with a 30-min handover call. Use this script:

> *"Now that we've delivered everything we quoted, I want to be honest about what comes next. AEO compounds over time — schema is deployed, GBP is rebuilt, citations are live, but AI engines need 30-90 days to fully crawl, index, and start citing you. That's normal.
>
> Two paths from here:
>
> **Self-manage:** You have everything, all logins, full documentation. Run it yourself. I'm available for hourly help at R750/hour if you need it.
>
> **Growth retainer:** I keep doing the compounding work — 2 articles/month, 4 GBP posts, schema updates, monitoring all 4 AI engines monthly, reporting on what's changing. R8,500/month, 3-month minimum, cancel anytime after.
>
> Most clients start retainer at handover because the schema and citations need ongoing maintenance to keep working. But it's your call — no pressure either way."*

About 30-50% convert to retainer at handover. The rest come back within 90 days because they realise self-management is harder than it sounded.

Document who said yes/no/maybe in your tracker. Follow up with the "no" group at day 30 and day 90 — at those moments their reasoning often changes.
