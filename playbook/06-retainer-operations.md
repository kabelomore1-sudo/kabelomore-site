# 06 — Retainer Operations (Growth + Premium)

How to deliver ongoing AEO work month after month so retainer clients stay 12+ months.

**Growth Retainer** = R8,500/mo, ~30-50 hours/month of work, 3-month minimum, cancel anytime after.
**Premium Retainer** = R15,000/mo, ~60-100 hours/month, includes month 1 implementation.

Critical principle: **retainers are sold once and renewed monthly by performance.** Every month a client is asking themselves "is this still worth R8,500?" Your job is to make the answer *obvious yes* via concrete delivery + visible reporting.

---

## Monthly cadence — what 30-50 hours looks like

The baseline Growth Retainer monthly delivery:

| Activity | Hours | Frequency |
|---|---|---|
| Researching, writing, publishing 2 detailed answer-shaped articles | 16-24 hrs | 2× per month |
| Writing + scheduling 4 GBP posts | 3-4 hrs | 4× per month |
| Updating structured data (schema markup) as new content lands | 2-4 hrs | Ongoing |
| Building 1-2 third-party citations | 3-5 hrs | Monthly |
| Monitoring AI citation rate across 4 engines + report | 4-6 hrs | Monthly |
| Tracking what client's competitors are doing | 3-4 hrs | Monthly |
| Strategy refinement, comms, project management | 2-4 hrs | Ongoing |
| **Total** | **30-50 hrs** | |

This is the description that lives on `/services` and is justified by the "Why monthly?" amber block. Match delivery to the description.

## The week-by-week cadence

A clean monthly rhythm beats an "ad-hoc when-I-feel-it" rhythm. Stick to this:

### Week 1 — Strategy + first article

- **Monday:** monthly planning. Pick the topic for article #1 + topic for #2. Identify any new citation opportunities. Decide GBP post themes for the month.
- **Tuesday-Wednesday:** research + write article #1 (~6-8 hrs). Use audit-agent for source data if relevant.
- **Thursday:** publish article #1 to client's blog. Deploy Article schema. Submit to Search Console.
- **Friday:** GBP post #1. Citation building start.
- **Status update:** Monday of Week 1.

### Week 2 — Citations + content engine

- **Monday-Tuesday:** complete 1-2 new citation submissions. Document each (screenshot URL).
- **Wednesday:** GBP post #2. Schema audit (anything new on the site? Is everything still validating?)
- **Thursday-Friday:** research + draft article #2.
- **Status update:** Thursday of Week 2.

### Week 3 — Article 2 + competitor monitoring

- **Monday:** publish article #2.
- **Tuesday:** GBP post #3.
- **Wednesday:** competitor monitoring run. Check what client's 3 competitors shipped this month (new pages, schema changes, new GBP posts, new content). Note in tracker.
- **Thursday-Friday:** any pending technical updates (schema fixes, sitemap refresh, llms.txt update if pricing or services changed).
- **Status update:** Monday of Week 3.

### Week 4 — Measurement + report

- **Monday:** GBP post #4.
- **Tuesday-Wednesday:** run the full 4-engine AI scan via audit-agent. Capture data. Compare to previous month's data.
- **Thursday:** write the monthly report (structure below).
- **Friday:** monthly report email to client + 30-min strategy review call (optional but recommended).
- **Status update:** integrated into the monthly report email.

Premium is priced as ~2x the work. Don't deliver 1.5x and hope they don't notice. Track hours and deliver the full ~60-100 hours/month or scope down honestly.

## When retainers go wrong (and how to recover)

### Symptom 1: client goes silent for 3+ weeks

You're sending status updates and getting no replies. Citation rate is moving but they don't see it.

**Recovery:** drop the formal updates. Send a 2-line WhatsApp: *"Hey [name], I've shipped [specific thing]. Quick 15-min call this week to walk through it together?"* Re-establish human contact before formality.

### Symptom 2: client asks "what am I paying for again?"

This is THE sign that your reporting isn't punching through. They're not seeing the work.

**Recovery:** schedule a 30-min "value reset" call. Bring the last 3 monthly reports. Walk through citation rate movement, competitor monitoring observations, content published. Then *recap the next 90 days plan.* This call saves more retainers than any tactical fix.

### Symptom 3: client wants to scale down to "every other month"

This is usually a budget issue, not a value issue. They like the work but cash flow is tight.

**Recovery:** offer a **Growth Retainer Lite** at R5,500/mo (1 article instead of 2, 2 GBP posts instead of 4, 1 citation per month instead of 1-2, monthly report). Don't lose the client — accept lighter scope.

### Symptom 4: client wants more than is in scope

They keep asking for landing pages, paid ad campaigns, social media management, design work — none of which is in Growth Retainer.

**Recovery:** quote each as an add-on. *"Growth Retainer covers AEO compounding. The landing page you want is a R4,500 add-on. Want to do it?"* Either they pay or they stop asking. Both fine.

### Symptom 5: client cancels after month 3

Almost always recoverable if you ask why. Sometimes it's: their business situation changed (out of your control). Sometimes it's: they didn't see results fast enough (educational gap — you didn't reset expectations on the 60-90 day compounding timeline). Sometimes it's: cheaper competitor pitched them.

**Recovery:** schedule a "exit interview" 30-min call. Listen. Don't pitch. Just understand. Often the client returns at month 6-9 once they've seen what happens without ongoing maintenance. The exit interview keeps the door open.

## The annual retention decision (month 11-12)

Around month 11, every retainer client gets the *renewal conversation*:

```
Subject: 12 months in — let's talk about year 2

Hi [CLIENT FIRST NAME],

Almost a year on the Growth Retainer. Quick reflection from my side
before we talk about year 2:

What worked:
- [SPECIFIC WIN 1]
- [SPECIFIC WIN 2]
- [SPECIFIC WIN 3]

What I'd do differently if starting over:
- [HONEST OBSERVATION 1]
- [HONEST OBSERVATION 2]

The compounding question for year 2:
Most clients see the biggest AEO returns in months 12-24, because
that's when citations compound and AI engines have re-indexed
multiple times. Stopping at month 12 leaves a lot of compounding
on the table.

Three options for year 2:

1. Continue at R8,500/mo (current rate, no change)
2. Increase to R10,500/mo with deeper scope (3 articles, 6 GBP
   posts, 1 strategic LinkedIn campaign per quarter)
3. Pause for 90 days and re-evaluate (you keep all assets, support
   window stays open)

Want to do a 30-min call this/next week to decide?

— Kabelo
```

The honest reflection is what wins year 2. Most consultants pretend everything was perfect. The good ones admit what they'd do differently. **That admission is what makes year 2 close.**

## Quarterly reviews (build in at month 3, 6, 9, 12)

Every 3 months, a 60-min strategic call (above and beyond the monthly review):

Agenda:
1. Where are we vs the original 6-month goal? (15 min)
2. What's the AEO landscape doing in their vertical? (15 min)
3. What's queued for the next 90 days? (15 min)
4. Any scope changes / add-ons / pivots? (15 min)

Document outputs in writing. Send a 1-page recap email after every quarterly review. **These calls are what separate retainers from project work in the client's mind.** They feel strategic, not transactional.

## Tracking + reporting infrastructure (set up once, reuse forever)

For every retainer client, maintain a Notion page (or Airtable / Google Sheets — whatever you actually use):

- Client info (contact, business basics, login refs)
- Monthly metrics history (citation rate, GBP impressions, articles published, citations added)
- Articles published log (with URLs)
- Citations log (directory + URL + date)
- Competitor moves observed (per month)
- Quarterly review notes
- Renewal date + renewal conversation log

This single document is what keeps retainer delivery from feeling chaotic. Pull from it for every monthly report. Update it weekly.

If you're doing 5+ retainer clients simultaneously, this becomes mandatory. Below 5, you can fake it. Above 5, you'll lose track without it.
