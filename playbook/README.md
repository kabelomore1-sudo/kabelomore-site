# Kabelomore — Operations Playbook

Internal reference. Not published on the public site. Single source of truth
for how AEO engagements get vetted, scoped, delivered, and compounded.

## Why this exists

Naval would say: *the highest-leverage hour you spend is the one that decides
how the next 100 hours get spent.* These docs are the decision layer that
sits above every client engagement, so:

- **Quality stays consistent** across clients regardless of when work happens
- **Scoping speed goes from hours to minutes** when a free scan converts
- **Future contractors / hires can deliver** without retraining from scratch
- **Selling gets easier** because you can crisply articulate what you do
- **Pricing stays defensible** because every quote follows the same logic

Use these like a chef uses a recipe book — not as a cage, but as a starting
point. Adapt to the client. Update the doc when reality teaches you something.

## Quick decision tree (use during a free scan call)

```
Free scan delivered  →  Follow-up call booked
            │
            ▼
    "What's their starting point?"
            │
   ┌────────┴────────┐
   │                 │
NO website        EXISTING website
   │                 │
   ▼                 ▼
Foundation     [Is it WORKING ok?]
Pack (12.5k)        │
or Lite (6.5k) ┌────┴────┐
               YES       NO/BROKEN
                │           │
                ▼           ▼
         Optimization   Foundation
         Pack (10.5k)   Pack rebuild
         or Lite (5.5k) (12.5k)
```

After once-off delivery, every client gets pitched **Growth retainer
(R8,500/mo)** — that's the recurring revenue compound. About 30-50%
will say yes. The 50% who don't still feed referrals + reviews.

## What's NOT in this playbook (yet)

- Pricing scoping calculator (currently inside `vetting-and-scoping.md`)
- Audit report template (build when first paying audit happens)
- Sales call scripts (build after 5 calls have happened — pattern emerges)
- Hiring/onboarding guide (build when first contractor gets hired)
- Quarterly review template for retainer clients (build at month 3 of first retainer)

When any of those become bottlenecks, write them. Not before.

## Naval's filter for adding to this playbook

Before you write a new doc, ask:
1. Have I done this thing 3+ times manually? (If less, don't templatize yet —
   you're guessing.)
2. Will I do this thing 5+ more times? (If less, the ROI on the doc is too low.)
3. Is the cost of getting this wrong > 30 minutes of cleanup? (If less, just wing it.)

Only when all three are YES does a thing become worth a playbook.
