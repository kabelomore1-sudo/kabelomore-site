# The Client Operations System

> What to do when a client signs. The operational manual that picks up
> where AUDIT-PLAYBOOK.md ends.
>
> Companion to:
> - `AUDIT-PLAYBOOK.md` — what you do for prospects (free scans, audits)
> - `LINKEDIN-PLAYBOOK.md` — daily distribution + content cadence
> - `CLIENT-OPERATIONS-SYSTEM.md` ← this file → what you do AFTER signature
>
> Goal: every retainer client gets the same predictable, premium delivery
> regardless of which tier they're on. Quality of execution = retention
> + referrals = how this business compounds.

## Stage 1 — Pre-Onboarding (signed but not started)

**Triggers**: Client returns signed agreement OR Stripe charge succeeds.

**Time-box**: 1 hour, all on day of signature.

### Within 1 hour of signature

```
[ ] Confirmation email sent (template below)
[ ] Invoice for first month sent (Stripe / Xero / manual)
[ ] Brief link sent (the matching /brief/[tier] URL)
[ ] Client added to CRM (Notion/Airtable) with all known fields
[ ] Slack channel / WhatsApp group created if applicable
[ ] Calendar invite for kickoff call sent (Lite: optional / Growth+: mandatory)
```

```
next:
```

The monthly rhythm AFTER month 1.

```

   Template: 200 words, what shipped Thursday-Sunday, what ships this week
   
Tue — Article writing day (Local Growth+) / GBP posts batch
   Local Growth: 1 article every 2 weeks, alternating with citation outreach
   AI Authority: 1 article weekly
   
Wed — LinkedIn ghost-writing batch (founder + company)
   Local Growth: 4 posts (1 founder + 3 company)
   AI Authority: 12 posts (3 founder + 5 company + 4 GBP)
   
Thu — Status update email (3-day cadence promised)
   
Thu/Fri — Citation outreach + reviews work
   Local Growth: 1-2 citations / week + 5 review responses
   AI Authority: ongoing citations + 10 review responses + 2 review campaigns
   
End-of-month Friday — Monthly performance report
   What was done, what moved, what's next, invoice for next month
```

## Stage 4 — The Crisis Playbook

What to do when something goes wrong fast.

### A. Negative review goes up

```
[ ] Don't react publicly within 24h
[ ] Notify client by WhatsApp within 1h
[ ] Draft response in Notion / shared doc — DO NOT publish without client approval
[ ] Flag to client: emotion vs facts split (what's true, what's exaggerated)
[ ] If brand-damaging: escalate to client's senior person + suggest legal review
[ ] If small/petty: respond professionally, never argue, deflect to private resolution
```

**Template response (always reviewed by client first)**:
```
Hi [REVIEWER NAME], thanks for the feedback. We take this seriously
and would like to understand what happened. Could you email us at
[email] so we can review and respond properly? — [CLIENT FIRM]
```

### B. Urgent client request comes in

```
[ ] Acknowledge within 1 hour: "Got it. Looking now."
[ ] Triage: is it scope-included, scope-extension, or escalation?
[ ] If scope-included: deliver same-day where possible, flag with timeline
[ ] If scope-extension: respond with effort/cost, ask for go/no-go
[ ] If genuine emergency (PR crisis, etc.): drop other work, charge as add-on
```

### C. Citation removal request (negative side)

If a citation suddenly disappears (delisting, shutdown of a directory):
```
[ ] Verify (other directories may show it differently)
[ ] If genuine removal: re-submit within 48 hours
[ ] Document why (if known) — feeds the body of work for future audits
```

### D. PR mention to amplify

If a client gets cited by Engineering News, Medical Brief, etc.:
```
[ ] Cross-post to LinkedIn (founder + company) within 24 hours
[ ] Add to website's "Featured in" section (build that section if missing)
[ ] Add to GBP post queue
[ ] Notify newsletter subscribers in next issue
[ ] Document for case study material
[ ] Use as PR for Kabelomore: "We helped [client] earn [mention]" (with permission)
```

## Stage 7 — Capturing Case Study Material

This is the IP loop closing. Every retainer client should be a potential
case study by month 6.

### When to ask

**Earliest**: month 6 (need 90 days of data to show meaningful change).

**Best timing**: when there's a quotable win — a journalist mention, a
new client they got from AI, a measurable citation lift, a retainer
upgrade.

### How to ask

```
Subject: [CLIENT] — case study opportunity

Hi [NAME],

Quick ask. The work we've done together has produced some meaningful
shifts:

  · [WIN 1 with number]
  · [WIN 2 with number]
  · [WIN 3 with number]

I'd love to write this up as a case study for kabelomore.com — fully
anonymised if you prefer (we'd say "a Pretoria medical practice"), or
named with your full attribution if you're comfortable.

What it would include:
  - Before/after AI visibility data (anonymised or named)
  - Specific tactics deployed (the work itself, no client business secrets)
  - Quantified outcomes
  - Quote from you (which you approve before publishing)

What I get: a third-party verifiable proof point.
What you get: a piece of public credibility we can both link to.

Up for it? Anonymous version if you'd rather, no problem either way.

— Kabelo
```

### Case Study Structure (when published)

Mirror the OMS Lifting Solutions case study structure already on
/case-studies/oms-lifting-solutions:
1. The business + their context
2. The starting state (Day 0 audit)
3. What we deployed (the methodology applied)
4. The outcomes (numbers, screenshots, citations)
5. Where they are now
6. Quote from the client
7. CTA — "Want similar results? [scan link]"

## The 12 Habits That Make Retainers Stick

After running enough retainers, the difference between churn and
retention isn't quality of work — it's predictability of communication.

1. **Always reply within 1 hour during business hours.** Even if the
   reply is "looking, will respond properly within X."
2. **Status update every 3 days, no excuses.** Even if "no progress —
   waiting on client" — say that.
3. **Show your work.** Every Friday, send the week's outputs (links,
   PDFs, screenshots). Don't make clients ask.
4. **Pre-empt questions.** If a client asks the same Q twice, write an
   FAQ for that client and link it next time.
5. **Surface bad news first.** "X didn't move this week, here's why and
   what we're doing." Trust compounds when you don't hide.
6. **Use named language.** "The Real Estate Method" + "Property 4 of 7"
   becomes shorthand. Anchors the relationship in shared vocabulary.
7. **Document everything publicly.** Every win = a LinkedIn post (with
   permission). Every learning = a newsletter teaser.
8. **Quarterly review or it didn't happen.** Don't let monthly become
   noise. The quarterly is where strategy actually lives.
9. **Charge correctly upfront.** Underpriced clients always become
   problem clients. If unsure, charge 30% more.
10. **Fire bad-fit clients.** Take the L. Bad-fit clients consume 3×
    energy and damage your average outcome metrics.
11. **Build the case study material from day 1.** Take baseline
    screenshots, capture before-state, log every action.
12. **Ask for the testimonial when the win happens, not 6 months later.**
    Hot iron rule.
