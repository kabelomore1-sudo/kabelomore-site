## Executive summary

Three products. Three different jobs.

- **Ubersuggest** is a *diagnosis funnel* — it shows you you're invisible, then paywalls the actionable detail. Optimised for self-serve SaaS conversion, not outcomes.
- **SEMrush** is an *enterprise SEO suite* with AI tracking bolted on — built for in-house SEO teams who already know what to do with the data and have the time to act on it.
- **kabelomore.com** is *the diagnosis plus the surgeon* — a free expert scan with verbatim AI answers and a prioritised fix list, with a human consultant who can either hand you the roadmap or execute it.

The sales wedge: **tools sell data; data has never deposited money into anyone's bank account. We sell the outcome.**

## What each one actually is

### Ubersuggest's AI Search Visibility tool

Source: 14 screenshots of the live product analysed in `2026-05-14-ubersuggest-ai-scan.md`.

The honest characterisation: **"see your problem, pay to see how bad it is."**

- First authenticated number is a stark **"0%"** + an empty Industry Rank dash. Designed as an emotional gut-punch.
- Competitor *names* (Hoist Factory, Elephant Lifting Equipment, etc.) are visible **for free** — but their *scores* are paywalled.
- The Top Prompts list is visible for free, intent-tagged (Informational / Navigational / Transactional). This is their single strongest feature.
- The Top Brands "Avg. rank" table is visible for top ~5 competitors, deeper rankings paywalled.
- Charts have **structure visible, values blurred** behind orange padlock + "Upgrade to unlock data" — maximum curiosity gap.
- ChatGPT is default; Gemini visible as a paid upsell banner ("Gemini now available!").
- Refresh: **monthly** ("Next AI Update Cycle in 30 days").
- No verbatim AI answers. No methodology. No remediation. No "near me" / local layer.

**Strength:** the prompt mining + intent classification is genuinely useful and shows real category-buyer language. We adopted this concept (Ticket 1, shipped) and made it our own.

**Weakness:** structurally cannot deliver outcomes. The model is designed to make you anxious enough to upgrade — it isn't designed to make you visible.

### SEMrush (AI visibility features within the broader suite)

*Honest framing: I have strong general knowledge of SEMrush as an industry-standard SEO platform, but I haven't run their specific AI visibility features against a project myself. The points below reflect what the platform is broadly known to do; verify specific feature names + outputs against your own testing before quoting them in public materials.*

- **Architecture:** ~40-tool enterprise SEO suite. AI/AEO features are recent additions layered onto a platform originally built for keyword research, backlink analysis, position tracking, technical site audits, content optimisation, and PPC research.
- **Pricing:** roughly Pro $139/mo, Guru $249/mo, Business $499/mo (annual discounts; no permanent free tier — only a 7-day trial). At the business level, often $1k+/mo with add-ons.
- **Who it's for:** in-house SEO teams + agencies. Assumes you have someone whose full-time job is interpreting the dashboards and translating output into work.
- **What it does well:** breadth, depth, data freshness, reporting/exports, position tracking across daily snapshots, backlink intelligence, competitive keyword discovery. Genuine power tool.
- **Where it falls down for an SMB buyer:**
  - **Cockpit, not driver.** It gives you the controls; it doesn't fly the plane. SMB owners who buy SEMrush typically use it once, then it sits there generating guilt and invoices.
  - **No remediation layer.** Same as Ubersuggest: tool gives data, you act on it. Outcomes are entirely on you.
  - **Global dataset, not SA-localised.** Citation directories, SA-specific signals (BBBEE, sector platforms like HelloPeter), and Pretoria-specific local nuance aren't first-class.
  - **No human-in-the-loop.** Pure self-serve.
  - **No GBP "nearby" fusion.** AI mentions are tracked in the abstract, not weighted against the prospect's local footprint.

**Strength:** if you already have an SEO team and need an industrial-grade data layer, SEMrush is the right tool. It is genuinely best-in-class at what it does.

**Weakness:** the buyer it's built for is not the kabelomore.com ICP. Industrial suppliers, medical practices, law firms in SA who want to be cited by AI engines don't have an in-house SEO team. They need someone to do the work, not someone to hand them a dashboard.

## The five things kabelomore does that neither tool does

These are the wedges. Memorise them.

1. **Verbatim AI answers.** Tools tell you you're at 0%. We show you the exact sentence the AI said when it recommended your competitor. Nothing makes a business owner move faster than reading their competitor's name in an AI answer where their own should be.

2. **Local / Regional / National tagging on every competitor.** Tools measure AI mentions in the abstract. We tag each surfacing competitor by how the AI itself situated them — `Local` means the supplier down the road is winning the answer and you aren't. That's a different (and more urgent, more fixable) problem than a national firm doing so. **No other tool in the comparison has this column.**

3. **A prioritised fix list mapped to fixed pricing.** Tools end where the work begins. We hand you 3 specific fixes ranked by impact ÷ effort, with each one pointing at a known package + price. You can act on the report yourself, or hand it to us.

4. **Methodology transparency per scan.** Tools hide how the score is computed. We disclose engine, date, prompt count, and known limitations *in the report*. Trust is a foundation, not a feature.

5. **Human review before delivery.** Every scan reviewed within 24 hours by a person who does this for a living. Tools never have judgment. We do.

## Where each tool wins (be fair — credibility matters in sales)

- **Ubersuggest** wins on price-of-entry curiosity (the free signup hooks SMBs). If a prospect just wants to see how broken they are with zero commitment, Ubersuggest will give them the 0% gut-punch fast.
- **SEMrush** wins on industrial-strength data layers — backlink intelligence, position tracking across keyword universes, technical site auditing, paid search research, content marketing. If a prospect is an *agency* selling SEO services and needs the data layer, SEMrush is the right tool.

**Neither wins on "make me visible." That's the gap we own.**
